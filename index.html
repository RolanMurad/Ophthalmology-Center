Frequently asked questions

  • Detailed computer vision diagnostics in our clinic are of two types: full examination - takes 2.5-3 hours and screening examination - takes 1 hour.
  • Cataracts not only need to be operated on, they need to be operated on! After all, cataracts significantly reduce the quality of life. Also, the more mature the cataract, the more complications it causes: long procedures and postoperative period, more risk of complications. Also, there are variants of cataract development that lead to irreversible loss of vision.
  • Sleep should be full. And it is advisable to sleep on a large, high pillow and after waking up immediately rise to reduce the pressure. Do not look at very bright light. In sunny weather it is best to wear sunglasses with UV protection. If desired, you can even use green glasses, as they calm the nervous system and reduce intraocular pressure.
  • The surgery itself takes about 10-15 minutes for both eyes, but patients spend a total of about 3-4 hours in the clinic. This includes preparation for the surgery, the surgery, the doctor's examination afterwards, and recommendations with medications.
  • During the first 1-2 weeks, it is desirable to reduce the visual load to 1-2 hours a day, with a gradual increase to 3-4 hours a day or more.
  • It does not matter what time of year you have the surgery. The main thing is to avoid colds in the first two weeks after surgery.
